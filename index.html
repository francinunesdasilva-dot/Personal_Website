Who I Am

Raised in the heart of Minas Gerais on a farm, I learned early on to navigate life with independence and curiosity. My journey has taken me across countries, languages, and experiences, shaping a life of resilience, exploration, and wonder.

I’m drawn to painting, meditation, nature, reading, and travel. Every moment is an opportunity to explore, reflect, and create a life full of discovery and meaning.

Travel

Over the years, I’ve explored 12 countries and visited 34 US National Parks. Each journey leaves me with new perspectives, stories, and inspirations that shape how I see the world and myself.

Travel isn’t just about destinations—it’s a lens to experience life, notice the beauty in landscapes, people, and fleeting moments, and carry those impressions back into daily living.

Paintings

Painting is how I give life to thoughts, feelings, and moments. Each work is a reflection of what I notice in myself and the world around me, captured through color, texture, and form.

Reflections

While Everything Moves

Look up at the sky and notice how the clouds keep moving…
After just a few seconds of watching them, they are no longer in the same place.

And inside—what is there?
Amid so many uncertainties, emptiness, and loneliness, everything is still in motion.
We may not yet know why, but movement is necessary.
Life is movement.

No matter how misaligned things may seem, they are, in truth, connected on an invisible plane.
Even if we don’t understand how, this movement was planned and is part of a greater journey;
a journey we are walking at every moment.

We don’t know where we are going, yet we continue forward, carried by movement itself.
What shapes our experience along the way are the choices we make.

The secret of life, then, lies in choosing what is right—
what makes sense—while we are in motion.

Tomorrow

I am running because I have a place to be
A place that is magical and sweet
Where pain shall not exist
But colorful balloons, dance, and light

I am rushing to get to this place
As I need to live in it
If there is something so beautiful
How could one live a different life otherwise?

But there is a cost to get to this place
A price that few are willing to pay
And that is for not living in the present
And perhaps in constant pain

The place I long for is called tomorrow.
I will keep on chasing tomorrow
Even though I don't know
if it will exist

Meditation

Meditation, for me, is one of the hardest things one can do, especially in the world we live in. The constant stimuli, chaos, and noise make it very difficult to sit quietly and observe the mind. As with anything in life, one becomes better with practice, yet even short periods of dedicated meditation are a challenge to truly examine and understand oneself from within.

Through daily practice and an 8-day silent retreat at a monastery, I’ve slowly learned the quiet power of presence, discovering clarity, stillness, and a deeper connection to my inner world.
