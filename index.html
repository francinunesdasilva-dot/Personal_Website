Franciele Nunes Da Silva

I have lived a few very different lives in one. I am drawn to hard work and clean simplicity and also to depth, wonder, and the kind of learning that changes you. I navigate life with curiosity, reflection, and a deep appreciation for moments that reveal meaning. This space captures my journey, passions, and the inspirations that shape my world.

Who I am

I grew up in Minas Gerais, Brazil, on a farm where life was simple, grounded, and closely connected to nature. Surrounded by fresh food, animals, and open land, I learned early lessons about hard work, community, and responsibility. As I became more aware of the financial challenges of that life, I knew I wanted to build a different future for myself. At thirteen, I left the farm, began working full time, and continued school part time. After high school, I earned a full scholarship to study architecture but eventually realized my path lay elsewhere. In 2017, I moved to the United States with no English and little certainty. I shifted my focus to business to understand how ideas turn into real ventures and to prepare for an entrepreneurial life. After college, I gained experience on Wall Street, broadening my perspective on business and strategy. One principle has remained constant throughout it all: stay curious, ask questions, and give full care to whatever you choose to build.

Travel

I travel for perspective and to come back with a calmer mind. Every journey brings new perspectives, unforgettable encounters, and moments that feed creativity and reflection. Travel helps me connect with the world and with myself, and the experiences I collect become fuel for both my professional and personal life. I have visited only 12 countries, but I have spent a lot of time in the US national parks and I have visited 34 of them.

Paintings

I started painting at a young age, paused for a long time, and recently resumed creating again. Painting is how I translate feelings, thoughts, and fleeting moments into color and form. Each piece is a fragment of my inner world expressed on canvas.

Meditation

Meditation is training and a harder one. In today's world it is very challenging to sit quietly for a longer period of time and examine what is going on inside. In 2025, I completed an intensive 8-day silent retreat at a remote monastery. Removing the noise of digital life revealed clarity, resilience, and a quiet strength that I carry back into my day to day.

Readings & Movies

I am very selective when it comes to books. I am drawn to psychology, philosophy, science, and biographies. Occasionally, a good novel makes its way into my list.

I started tracking the movies I watch in 2024, even though I have watched far more than what appears on my list. I love going to the movie theater and watching movies hot the press. All genres are welcome except for the really terrifying horror ones.

Floki

Floki is my constant companion and a source of joy in my life. He reminds me to slow down, enjoy the little moments, and approach each day with playfulness. He loves walks, but more importantly, he is a big fan of car rides.
